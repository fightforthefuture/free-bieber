Who's Justin Bieber?

He's an American teen pop celebrity who got discovered on the internet. For more information than you could possibly want, visit wikipedia, or ask a seven year old, any seven year old.

History of Congress against dudes singing

Copyright has been used to sue: the girlscouts for singing around a campfire. people singing "Happy Birthday" in a restaurant. DJ Drama for releasing records Lil' Wayne asked him to.

A kid, singing a song

This is a video of child celebrity Justin Bieber singing "So Sick" by the artist Neyo. YouTube videos like this one were what made him famous. Lots of kids do this for fun, and lots of artists get started this way.

5 years in jail?

A new law would make this a felony, with a five-year jail sentence. for any video with more than a handful of views. Other "crimes" may include: posting video with incidental background music (even just a cellphone ringing!), videos of your kids in a play, or your own video of a sports event.

Nice one, Congress

Both Democrats and Republicans usually go along with the music and movie companies, even Republicans from conservative places where "Hollywood" is a dirty word. Funny, that. It's because they're scared of the lobbyists' money and they think voters don't care.

Huh? It's illegal?

Copyright law is so crazy, just singing somebody else's song in public without permission could be infringement. Because he and his mom posted them to advance his music career, it's _commercial_ infringement. Ridiiculous, but true.

Who's idea was this?

The music and movie companies'. In their eyes, stricter copyright law = more $$. They liked it better when peope paid $18 for _their_ stuff on plastic discs rather than watching wonderful stuff made by everybody online. They tried to ban the VCR ([true!]) and they'd like to turn the internet into cable TV.

What should we do?

Should we stopped putting videos of our kids and families singing songs on the internet? Of course not! But if we don't stop laws like this, undeserving people are going to get hurt. Remember: these laws happen because they think you don't care. So... tell them you care
