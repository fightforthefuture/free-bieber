FREE BIEBER — Justing Bieber faces 5 brutal years in prison.

Could This Really Happen?

Congress is about to vote on a bill that would make posting videos of songs and other content a felony -- with up to 5 years in prison.

But wait... didn't Justin Bieber get famous by posting YouTube videos of him singing copyrighted songs? That's right, he certainly did, and those videos are still on his YouTube account. If this bill passes, he'll be committing a felony, and he could get 5 years in jail.

The Prosecution's case

Once Congress passed S. 978, harmless uses of copyrighted work with more than 10 views became potential felonies: clips, background music, cover songs.

Prosecutors proved Bieber benefited commercially, and that was easy--the videos advanced his music career. The defense claimed fair use, but the judge, disagreeing, handed down the maximum sentence: 5 years.

Bieber arrested

Just after 3pm PST, Bieber posted to his Twitter account, "OMG-- LAPD at house, want to arrest bieb re: neyo songs PLS RT!!"

By 3:12pm, a now-routine "singing online" arrest had become a full-scale tweenage riot, and a SWAT team was deployed to extract Bieber from throngs of small but enraged fans.

Three officers were bitten in ensuing violence, one was hospitalized.

Selena Gomez visitation

Bieber girlfriend Selena Gomez made an appearance during Tuesday’s visiting hours to show support for the jailed pop star.

The Wizards of Waverly Place star herself is facing charges over an unauthorized dressing room version of Superbass by rapper Nicki Minaj.

When asked if she regretted recording the video, Gomez responded, “Oh f--- no, I killed that s---.”
